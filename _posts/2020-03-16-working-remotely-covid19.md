First, a little disclaimer: what I wrote here is not meant to be a lesson on how you should behave during a quarantine period like this one. This is the way I lead my life and manage my time.

## The emergency

This is my first post on this blog and it's not a tech post. It sounds strange since this is a technical blog, but I think that what is happening during COVID-19 emergency deserves a little chat. 
I live in Italy and work at an IT Enterprise as a Software Engineer. My company is located in Padova, Veneto. The emergency started in a town near Padova, therefore my managers decided to let us working remotely. This happened on **24 February**.

![The first days of COVID-19 emergency](https://images.agi.it/pictures/agi/agi/2020/03/08/100722863-c39d0ff0-f050-4de5-b571-6e59bf461180.jpg "First zones with emergency measures for COVID-19")

Three weeks have passed and since then the global situation got worse and now the **whole country is under quarantine at least till 3 April** ([Coronavirus: Italy extends emergency measures nationwide](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-51810673)). 

## My new routine

After the very first week of remote working, when I understood that the quarantine period could have been stretched for another month, I began planning my routine in differently. Here it is:

* 07:30 Wake up
* 07:30-07:45 Breakfast with a large cup of coffee, greek yoghurt and oatmeal
* 07:45-07:55 Beard shaving and teeth cleaning
* 08:00 Turn on my PC and login for using the company's VPN: work begins
* 08:00-12:30 Working
* 12:30 - 13:30 Light lunch and walk outside (only on the terrace)
* 13:30 - 17:00 Working
* 17:00 - 18:00 Workout: Yoga (warmup), Crossfit and stretching
* 18:00 - 19:30 Reading, gaming, watching movies, learning, news
* 19:30 or 20:00 - 20:30 Dinner 
* 20:30 - 23:00 - Reading, gaming, watching movies and getting in touch with my friends and my girlfriend through phone calls or videocalls
* 23:00 - Go to bed and around 23:30 I usually fall asleep

Lunch and dinner are always light since I'm sitting on my chair the whole day long. I'm mitigating this by working out every day or whenever I can, and by standing up when I need to call some colleague or by taking small breaks during work time. The first week I could go out for a walk but soon the emergency measures forced me to stay at home. This is partially overcome by staying outside on my terrace and listening to audiobooks or some music. The workout is the best moment of the day and a lot of people are sharing their workout routine on social networks such as Instagram, so I sometimes try new exercises following what other people did. It's easy enough to come up with a homemade training that keeps you fit and well. It is very important to me because of my work and especially Yoga sessions help prevent postural issues and act well as mind relaxation too. I wanna recommend [this YouTube channel called Breathe and Flow](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCbfPq-uRqonJQli41muSLeQ) that I use to do my daily Yoga practices. This is really great. I enjoy reading especially before falling asleep. I'm currently reading *Memories of Ice* by Steven Erikson and I'm loving it. I usually read books on my Kindle but when I'm not travelling and if I'm comfortable enough I prefer printed books. Please feel free to [follow me on Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/user/show/62728165-pbro-94) so we can share some thoughts.

In my spare time, I also do some research and learn something new. I'm keeping up with Rust (I just love it!!!), Go and Elixir since these are the programming languages that I want to keep my focus on during this year. Maybe I'll write a specific post about how I learn new programming languages. ## Thoughts on full remote working

This situation is completely new to me. Italian IT companies always underestimated the power of remote working and never took advantage of it. Nevertheless, a lot of them now think that this is a valid practice that brings benefits to all, both employees and employers. When it all started I wasn't so happy on working remotely since I thought that I worked better by being close to my colleagues. After the first week I shifted my opinion.
My perspective on this is that I can optimize my time and be more productive since I can work without all the noise and disturbance of the open-space. It is necessary to use some VoIP technology and for what concerns my company it seems that Microsoft Teams works very well for this task. Finally, I can spend more time with my family and manage to organize my spare time better than before.

## Wrapping up

I think that this emergency will permanently change the way we look at work. For what concerns Italy, its people are responding well to the emergency measures and medical staff all across the country is doing a great job managing this situation. We all need to change our behaviour when other people's lives are in danger. Adopting something like remote working we are thus helping decreasing chances that COVID-19 will spread more than usual. I changed a little bit my daily routine, but this will result in a great change for others.
